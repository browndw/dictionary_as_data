Standard or Estandard, (from the Fr. Estandart) an Ensign for Horsmen in War, and is commonly taken for that of the King, or chief General. It is also used for the principal or standing measure of the King, to the scantling whereof, all the measures throughout the Land are, or ought to be framed, &c. Magna Charta, and confirmed 14 Edw 3. cap. 12. Of these Standards and Measures, read Britton cap. 30.

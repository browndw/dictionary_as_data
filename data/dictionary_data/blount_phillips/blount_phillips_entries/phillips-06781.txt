Julius, the sirname of Ascanius, the son of Aeneas, and his first wife Creusa, he came along with his father into Italy, and built the City Alba in the place, where he found the white Sow with the 30 Pigs: also the son of Ascanius, who stood in competition for the kingdom of Alba, with Silvius Posthumus the son of Aeneas by his second wife Lavinia; the word signifieth in Greek, the soft down appearing on the chin of a young man, before he comes to have a perfect beard.

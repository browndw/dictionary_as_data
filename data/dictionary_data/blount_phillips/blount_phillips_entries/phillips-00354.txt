Agesilaus, a King of the Lacedemonians, who overthrew Tissapa cent ernes the Persian General, at the River Pactolus, and the Athenians and Boeotians at Coronea; also a stout Athenian, and brother of Themistocles; he being judged to be sacrificed at the Altar of the Sun, for killing Mardonius, held his hand a good while in the fire without changing his countenance, and affirmed that all the Athenians were such as himself, whereupon he was set free.

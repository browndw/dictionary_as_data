Hua cent ngs, may seem to come from the French Haul-ser, i. to raise, elevate or sit aloft; for it signifies the principal and highest Court in London. An. 11. H. 7. cap. 21. and Fitzh. nat. br. fol. 23. See An. 9. Ed. 1. Cap unico. Other Cities and Towns also have had a Court of the same name, as Winchester, Lincoln, York, Sheppey, &c. where the Barons or Citizens have a Record of such things as are determinable before them. Flet. l. 2. c. 55.

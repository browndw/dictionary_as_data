Furlong (of two Fr. words, Fort-long, i. very long, or quasi furrow-long) is a quantity of ground, containing twenty Lugs, Roods, or Poles in length, and every Pole sixteen foot, and a half, eight of which Furlongs make a mile, An. 35. E. 1. ca. 6. It is otherwise the eighth part of an Acre. See Acre. In the former signification the Romans call it [Stadium], in the latter [Jugerum] This measure which we call a Pole, is also called a Perch, and differs in length, according to the custom of the Country. See Perch, Stade.

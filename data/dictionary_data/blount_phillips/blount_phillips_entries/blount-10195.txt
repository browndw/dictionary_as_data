Suspension (suspensio) a hanging up; also doubt, incertainty of minde. It is used for a temporal stop of a mans right, and differs from Extinguishment in this, that a right of estate suspended, revives again, but extinguished, dies for ever. Brook tit. Extinguishment, and Suspension. f. 314. Supension is also sometimes used in our Common, as it is in the Cannon Law, pro minori Excommunicatione. An. 24. H. 8. c. 12.

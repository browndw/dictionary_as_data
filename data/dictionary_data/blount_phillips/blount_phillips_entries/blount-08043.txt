Pia cent rim (peregrinus) one that travels into strange Countreys, commonly taken for him that goes in devotion to any holy place; a Pilgrim and a Palmer differeth thus; the Pilgrim had some dwelling place, the Palmer none: The Pilgrim travelled to some certain place, the Palmer to all, and not to any one in particular; The Pilgrim might go at his own charge, the Palmer must profess wilful poverty; The Pilgrim might give over his profession, the Palmer must be constant, till he had obtained the Palm; that is, victory over his spiritual enemies, and life, by death Chauc.

Heretoga (Sax.) was our antient appellation for the cheif Conductor of an Army, for which we have long since used our borrowed French word Duke, from the Latin Dux. Here in the antient Teutonick, is an Army, and toga signifies to draw or train forward. The Netherlanders for Duke do yet use the name Herrogh, and the Germans write it Herirog. Verst.

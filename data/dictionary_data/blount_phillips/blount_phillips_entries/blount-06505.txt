Merchenlage, was one of the three sorts of Laws out of which the Conqueror framed ours, mingled with those of Normandy. Cam. Brit. p. 94. who also p. 103, shews, that in An. 1016. this land was divided into three parts, whereof the West Saxons had one, governing it by the Laws called West Saxon Laws, or West-Saxonlaeg, and that contained these nine Shires, Kent, Southsex, Southrey, Barkshire, Hampshire, Wiltshire, Sommerset, Dorset, and Devonshire.

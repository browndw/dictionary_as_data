Manna, a certain delicious food wherewith God fed the children of Israel in their journey to Canaan, being a congealed dew which fell from Heaven, it comes from the Hebrew word Manah, i. to distribute or Mahna, i. what is it, because they admired what it was: there is also at this day a certain sweet dew, which falling early in the morning upon trees and herbs, is called Manna, which congealing into a whitish substance, is of a pleasant taste, and is much used in physick.

Innes of Court, 4 houses or Celledges, for the intertainment of Students of the law, namly, the two Temples, Inner and Middle, which were anciently the habitations of the Templars, or Knights of Jerusalem, (to which was added the outward Temple, which is now called Essex House) Lincoln's Inne, built by Henry Lacy, Earl of Lincoln, for his own dwelling house, and Grais Inne, anciently the mannour house of Baron Gray, in the time of Edward the third.

Remembrancers, three officers belonging to the Exchequer; the first is called the Kings Remembrancer, who entreth in his office all recognisanses taken before the Barons, and maketh bonds for any of the Kings debts, or for appearance or observing of orders, and maketh proces for the breach of them. The second the Lord Treasurers Remembrancer, who puts him and the rest of the Justices in remembrance of such things as are to be dealt in for the Princes behoof. The third is the Remembrancer of the first fruits and tenths, who taketh all composition for first fruits and tenths, and maketh process against such as pay not the same.

Landskip (Belg.) Parergon, Paisage, or By-work, which is an expressing the Land, by Hills, Woods, Castles, Valleys, Rivers, Cities, &c. as far as may be shewed in our Horizon. All that in a Picture which is not of the body or argument, is Landskip, Parergon, or By-work. As in the Table of our Saviours Passion, the picture of Christ on the Rood (which is the antient English word for Cross) the two Theeves, the blessed Virgin Mary, and St. John, are tht Argument: But the City Jerusalem, the Country about, the clouds, and the like, are Landskip. El. Arm.

Assart (Fr. Essarter) to glade or make glades in a wood; also to grub up or clear a ground of bushes, shrubs, &c. or to lop off the boughs of a Tree. Assart is taken for an offence committed in the Forest, by plucking up those woods by the roots, that are Thickets or Coverts of the Forest, and by making them plain, as arable land. Manwoods For. Law. But if a man sue out a Licence to assart his grounds in the Forest, and to make it several for Tillage, then it is no offence. Cow.

Chrisome (a in non-Latin alphabet a) signifies properly the white cloth, which is set by the Minister of Baptism upon the head of a child newly annointed with Chrism after his Baptism: Now it is vulgarly taken for the white cloth put about or upon a child newly Christned, in token of his Baptism; wherewith the women use to shrowd the child, if dying within the month; Otherwise it is usually brought to Church at the day of Purification.

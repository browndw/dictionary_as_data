Arthur, a Latine name in Iuvenal drawn from the goodly fixed Star Arcturus, and that from Arctus, is the Bear, as AEisicinus among the Romans. The famous Arthur made this name first famous among the Britains. Cam. But why may not Arthure be rather a Brittish word composed of Arta cent , which signifies a Bear, and awr, signifying a man, Vir? So Arthur, quasi, a man that for his strength and terror may be called a Bear.

Sconce (from the Teut. Schana cent s) a Block-house or Fortification in War; also taken for the Head, because a Sconce or Block-house is made, for the most part round in fashion of a Head, whence comes the term in Oxford, to Sconce one (Lat. Mulctare pecunia) i. to set up so much in the Buttery Book upon his head, to pay, as a punishment for his offence committed. Min.

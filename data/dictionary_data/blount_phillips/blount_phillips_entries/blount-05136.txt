H.S. This Character, compounded of two Capital II. and the letter S. thus coupled together, signifies Sestertius, i. a silver coyn among the Romans consisting of two Asses and a half, whence it has the name Sestertius, quasi semistertius, i. two and half the third: and therefore you see the numeral note of two, and S for semis, which is half; it is the fourth part of the Roman Denarius, and in value among us three half pence farthing. q.

Larceny (from the French Larcin, i. theft) is defined by West. part. 2. Symb. tit. Inditements, to be theft of personal Goods or Chattels in the owners absence; and in respect of the things stoln, it is either great or small. Great Larceny is, when the things stoln, though severally, exceed the value of twelve pence; and Petit Larceny is, when the Goods stoln exceed not the value of twelve pence. Hitherto Mr. West. But he differs from Bracton lib. 3. Tract. 2. ca. 32. num. 1. Of this see more in Stawnf. Pl. Cor. l. 1. ca. 15, 16, 17, 18, 19.

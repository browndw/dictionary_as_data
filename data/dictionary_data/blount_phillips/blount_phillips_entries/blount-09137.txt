SAbaoth, Sabbath, Sabbaoth or Sabboth (Lat. Sabbatum, from the Hebrew, a in non-Latin alphabet a, Scabath, (i. quievit) a day of rest, or a time set apart for holy rest, which is Friday among the Turks, Saturday among the Jews, and Sunday, or our Lords day, among us Christians; which instead of the Saturday or Jewish Sabbath, was instituted by the Apostles to be kept festival, in honor and memory of Christs Resurrection on that day; and so hath been observed ever since; see Moses & Aaron. p. 97.

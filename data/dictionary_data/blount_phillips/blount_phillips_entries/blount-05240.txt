Impost (Fr.) Imposition, Tallage, Custome, Tribute; and more particularly it signifies the tax received by the Prince for such Merchandises as are brought into any haven from other Nations. Ann. 31. Eliz. c. 5. And I think it may in some sort be distinguished from Custom, because Custom is rather that profit which the Prince makes of wares shipped out of the Land; yet they may be confounded.

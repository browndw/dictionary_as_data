Lincoln, the chief Citie of Lincolnshire, anciently called Lindum, and by the Brittains Lindecoit, by Bede Lindecollina civitas, from the old Brittish La cent in, as Cambden is of opinion, which signifieth a Lake, it being situate near a Lake, and upon a hill, in this Citie Vortimer, the scourge of the Saxons ended his dayes.

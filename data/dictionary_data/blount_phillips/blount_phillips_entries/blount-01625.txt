Carrucate of Land (carrucata terrae) is a word much used in the antient Charters, and Land-evidences of this nation, and signifies as much land as may be tilled in a year by one Plough; it is also called in the antient Laws Hilda vel Hida terrae, and in others Carue de terre; now a Plough-land. Carrucata is a corruption from the French CarruA<<, a Plough.

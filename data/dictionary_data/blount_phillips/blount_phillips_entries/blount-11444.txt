When an Action of Debt or Detinue is brought against one (upon some secret agreement or contract had between the parties, without Specialty shewed, or other matter of Record) for goods lent or left with the Defendant, then the Defendant may Wage his Law, if he will, that is, Swear upon a Book, and certain persons with him, that he detains not the Goods, nor ows any thing to the Plaintiff in manner and form as he hath declared. Terms of the Law. Note that the offer to make the Oath is called Wager of Law, and when it is accomplished, it is called the doing your Law.

Meteor (Gr. a in non-Latin alphabet a, i. sublimis) an apparition, or any imperfect mixt substance ingendred on high, in the air; as Rain, Snow, Thunder, Hail, Lightning, Blazing-Stars, Clouds and Wind; all which are made of vapors or exhalations drawn up from the Earth and Sea, by the attractive vertues of the Sun. Vide Aristot. Meteorolog.

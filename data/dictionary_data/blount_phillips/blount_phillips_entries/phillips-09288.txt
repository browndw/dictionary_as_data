Plimouth, a famous Port Town in Cornwell, so called as it were the mouth of the River Plime: in this place, the fable goeth that Corinoeus threw down the Gyant Gogmogog from a steep Rock, it was anciently called Sutton, and was divided into two parts, Sutton Prior, as belonging to the Priors, and Sutton Vautort belonging to the Vautorts, stiled in old Records de Valle Terta.

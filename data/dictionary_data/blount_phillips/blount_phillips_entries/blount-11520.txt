Year (annus) was divided by Julius Caesar into twelve moneths, fifty two weeks, three hundred sixty and five dayes, and six hours, wanting eleven minutes, which odd six hours, every Leap or fourth year, make the odd day in February. The moneth is the twelfth part of the Solary year, the week is neer the fourth part of the moneth; the natural week day, a seventh part of the week, the Quadrant the fourth part of the day, the hour the sixth part of the Quadrant; the Point the fourth part of the hour; the Moment or Minute about the fifteenth part of the Point, &c. The Greeks observed the Lunary year, that is twelve revolutions of the Moon, three hundred fifty and four dayes. Br.

Legion, (lat.) a Company of Souldiers, among the ancient Romans, consisting of three, or four thousand foot, and three, or four hundred horse, afterwards being increased to six thousand foot, and six hundred horse, every ordinary foot Legion consisting of ten Cohorts, every Cohort of three Maniples, every Maniple of two Centuries, & each Century of a 100 men, likewise every horse Legion contained six Turmae, or Troupes, every Troup ten Decuries, and every Deca cent ry ten men; also there are in every Legion four Orders of Souldiers, the Triaria cent , the Principes, the Hastati, or Spearmen, the Velites, or light-armed.

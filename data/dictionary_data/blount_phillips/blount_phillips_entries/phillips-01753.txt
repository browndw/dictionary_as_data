Bristow, the name of a pleasant Citie, standing partly in Sommerset-shire, partly in Gloster-shire, it is so called as it were Brightstow, which in the Saxon signifieth a bright, or shining place, in British it was called Caer Oder Nant Badon, i. e. the Citie Oder in the Vale of Badon, it was fortified by Robert Bishop of Constance, against King William Rufus, with a Wall, which this day is in part standing.

Swithin (Sax.) from the old English Switheahn, that is, very high, as Celsus or Exuporius with the Romans. This name hath been taken up in honor of St Swithin, the holy Bishop of Winchester, about the year 860, and called the weeping St Swithin, for that about his Feast Praesepe and Aselli, rainy Constellations, arise cosmically, and commonly cause rain. Cam.

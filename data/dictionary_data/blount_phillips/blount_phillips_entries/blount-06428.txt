Medalia Medal (Fr. medaille) an antient and flat Jewel, or a peice of antient coyn or plate, wherein the figure of some notable person, or some ingenious device is cast or cut; examples of the first are common; of the later, we read, that when some dislikes grew between the English and States of the United Provinces, they, fearing it might tend to the hurt of both, caused to be imprinted upon a Medalia, two pitchers floating on the water with this motto, Si collidimur, frangimur. Cam.

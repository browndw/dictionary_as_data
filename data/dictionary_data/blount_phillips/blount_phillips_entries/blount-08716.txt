Rave (rapus vel rapa) is a part of a County, signifying as much as an Hundred; as Southsex is divided into six parts; which by a peculiar name are called Rapes, viz. The Rape of Chichester, of Arundel, of Brember, of Lewis, of Puensey, of Hastings. Cam. Brit. p. 225. Whom also see pag. 229. These parts are in other places called Tythings, Lathes, or Wapentakes. Smith de Rep. Angl. lib. 2. c. 16.

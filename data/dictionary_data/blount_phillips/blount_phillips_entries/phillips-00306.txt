Aesculapius, the son of Apollo and Coronis, he, being taught by Chiron the art of Physick, restor'd Hippolitus the son of Theseus to life, who, because the had refus'd the embraces of Phaedra his Mother-in-law, was by her meanes torn in pieces by wild Horses, but Jupiter was so incens't at this cure that he slew Aesculapius with a thunderbolt.

Sirens, certain Sea-deities, three in number, Parthenope, Ligea, and Leucosia, the daughters of Achelous and Calliope, having their upper part like maids, and their lower parts like fishes; they used by the sweetnesse of their voices to allure Mariners to the rocks and cause them to be cast away: which Vlisses foreseeing, stopt the ears of his associates with soft wax, and caused himself to be bound to the mast of the ship; whereupon they seeing themselves contemned, cast themselves headlong into the Sea.

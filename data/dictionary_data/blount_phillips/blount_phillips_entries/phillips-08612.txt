Osiris, the son of Jupiter and Niobe the daughter of Phoroneus, whom he succeeded in the Kingdom of Argos, but after a while leaving that Kingdom to his brother Aegialeus, he went and subdued Aegypt, and married the daughter of Inachus called Io or Isis, who first taught the Egyptians Letters, and sundry Arts and Sciences; he was at length slain by Typhon his brother, and after his death was worshipped by the Egyptians in the shape of an Oxe.

Julius Caesar, the first of the Roman Emperours, he subdued France, Spain, Brittain, the Low Countries, and the greatest part of Germany, afterwards he entred into a civil war with Pompey, whom he utterly defeated at the battle of Pharsalia, and his two sons, Cneus and Sextus Pompeius in Spain; and having reigned three years in Rome, as absolute Emperour, he was at length slain in the Senate house, by Brutus and Cassius, the word signifies in Greek softhaired.

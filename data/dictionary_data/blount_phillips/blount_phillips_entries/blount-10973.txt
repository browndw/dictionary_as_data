Vatican (vaticanus) the chief Library in Rome, called also the Palatine, founded by Pope Sixtus the fourth, who not onely stored it with the choisest books he could pick out of Europe, but allowed also a large revenue for its perpetual augmentation. It is so called from the Hill Vatican, where the Library stands; And that Hill was so called from a cent aticinium, i. a foretelling; because it was famous for many divinations and prophesies uttered upon it.

Wilton, a Town of Wiltshire, in ancient times the principal Town of the whole Shire, and from which it took its denomination, that it was heretofore called Ellandunum, appears by the Testimony of old Records, when Weolstan being stiled Earle of Ellandunum; it is farther added, that is to say of Wilton, here in a very bloudy battel, Egbert King of the West-Saxons, overcame Beorwulf, King of Mercia, in the year of our salvation 821. here also about fifty years after King Aelfrid, joyning battel with the Danes, was at length put to the worst.

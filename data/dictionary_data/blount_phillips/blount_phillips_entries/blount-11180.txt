They were greatly honored in the City, and had divers priviledges; for they were carried in Chariots, and the chiefest Magistrates would do reverence to them; they had Officers going before them, as the Consuls had, and if they met any one, who was led to be put to death, they had authority to deliver him, taking an oath that they came not that way of purpose; they might also make a Will, and dispose of their Goods as they pleased; but if any of them were found to live unchaste, she was openly carried with sad silence to the Gate called Collina, where, being put into a deep pit, she was presently buried alive. These Vestals were first instituted by Numa Pompilius, or (as some write) by Romulus.

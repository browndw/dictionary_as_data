Surplusage (Fr. Surplus) signifies in our Common Law superfluity or addition more then needs, which sometimes is a cause that the writ abates. Brook tit. Nugation and Superfluity fol. 100. Plowden casu Dive and Maningham f. 93. b. It is also sometimes applied to matter of account, and signifies a greater disbursment then the charge of the Accomptant amounts to.

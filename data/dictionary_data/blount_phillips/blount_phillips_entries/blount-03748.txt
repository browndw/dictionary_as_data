Essoine, comes of the Fr. essoynA(C) or exoinA(C), i. causarius miles, he that hath his presence forborn or excused upon any just cause, as sickness or other incumbrance. It signifies in our Common Law an alleadgement of an Excuse for him that is summoned or sought for to appear or answer to an Action real, or to perform suit to a Court Baron, upon just cause of absence, &c. See more in Cowel.

Secession (secessio) a departing from other, a forsaking or going aside; a separating ones self from another. In antient Rome it was a general Insurrection and revolt of the Commons, wherein they left the City, until such time as they had the authority of their Tribunes strengthned; yea, and certain Laws enacted and established by a solemn oath, with a curse denounced against all those that went about to abrogate or abolish the same, which thereupon were called Sacratae Leges. Livy.

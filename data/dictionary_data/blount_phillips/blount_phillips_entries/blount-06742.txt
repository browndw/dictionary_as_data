Mortgage (Fr. mort. i. mors, and gage, i. pignus, merces) signifies in our Common Law, a pawn of Land or Tenement, or any thing movable laid or bound for money borrowed, peremptorily to be the Creditors for ever, if the mony be not repaid at the day agreed on; And the Creditor holding such Land or Tenement is called Tenant in Mortgage.

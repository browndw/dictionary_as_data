Poynings Law, is an Act of Parliament made in Ireland. 10 Hen. 7. and was so called, because Sir Edw. Poynings was Lieutenant of Ireland when that Law was made; whereby all the Statutes of England were made of force in Ireland. For before they were not, neither are any now in force in Ireland, which were made in England since that time. Cokes 12 Rep. fol. 109.

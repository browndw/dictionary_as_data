Heriot alias Hariot (heriotum) in the Saxon Heregeat from here, i. exercitus) it signified in our Saxon time a tribute given to the Lord, for his better preparation toward war. The name is still retained, but the use altered; for whereas by Mr. Lamberts opinion, it signified as much as Relief doth now with us; it is taken now for the best Chattel that the Tenant hath at the hour of his death, due to the Lord by custom, be it Horse, Oxe, or any such like.

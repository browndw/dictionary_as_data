Coffa or Cauphe, a kind of drink among the Turks and Persians, (and of late introduced among us) which is black, thick and bitter, destrained from Berries of that nature, and name, thought good and very wholesom: they say it expels melancholy, purges choler, begets mirth, and an excellent concoction. Herb. tr. 150. nat. hist. 155.

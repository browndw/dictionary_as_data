Glocester, the chief City of Glocestershire, it was called by the Saxons Glevecester, in Latin Glevum, by the Brittains Caer-Glove, i. fair City: It is also called by some Claudia-Cestria, from the Emperour Claudius, who as it is fabulously reported, married his daughter Genissa to Arviragus the Brittish King.

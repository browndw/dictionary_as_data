Friar or Frier (from the Lat. Frater, or from the Fr. frA(C)re, i. a brother) there are four principal Orders reckoned of them An. 4. H. 4. ca. 17. (viz.) Minors, Grey Friers, or Franciscans; Augustins; Dominicans, or Black Friers; and White Friers or Carmelites; from these four Orders the rest descended. See in Zecchius de Repuh. Eccl. p. 380. And Linwood, tit. de Relig. Domibus, ca. 1. verbo, St. Augustin.

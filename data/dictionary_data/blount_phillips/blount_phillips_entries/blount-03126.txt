Dismes (decimae) Tythe, or the tenth part of all the fruits, either of the earth or beasts, or our labor due to God, and consequently to him that is of the Lords lot, and hath his share, viz. our Pastor. It signifies also the tenths of all spiritual Livings, yearly given to the Prince, called a perpetual Disme. A. 2. and 3. Ed. 6. ca. 35. which in antient times were paid to the Pope, till Pope AErban gave them to Richard the second, to aid him against Charls the French King, and those other that upheld Clement the seventh against him. Pol. Virg. l. 20. Lastly, It signifies a tribute levied of the temporality. Holinshed. fol. 111.

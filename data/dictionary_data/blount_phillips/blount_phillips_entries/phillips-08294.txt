Nottingham, the chief Town of Nottinghamshire, it is so called by a mollified pronuntiation, from the Saxon word Snottengaham, i. a house of Dennes, or Caves, it hath a strong Castle, which the Danes held out against Aethered, King of the West-Saxons, and his brother Aelfrid, who were stirred up by Burthred, King of the Mercians against them.

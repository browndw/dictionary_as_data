Carlile, an ancient City in Cumberland, almost encompast with the Rivers Eden, Peteril, and Cand, it was called by the Romans Luguballia, by the ancient Brittains Caerlnalid, Egfrid, King of Northumberland, made a deed of gift of it to St. Cuthbert: this City being depopulated by the Danes, and lying buried in Rubbish for 2000 years, began to flourish again in the time of William Rufus, by whom it was first repaired.

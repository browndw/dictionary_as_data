Stil-yard or Steel-yard (Guilda Teutonicorum, An. 32. H 8. c. 8. and An. 32. ejus. ca. 14.) is a place near the Thames in London, where the Fraternity of the Easterling Merchants, or the Merchants of Hanse and Almain (Ann. 1. Edw. 6. cap. 13.) were wont to have their abode. It is so called of a broad Yard or Court, wherein Steel was much sold, upon which that house is founded; see Hanse. The place is now onely famous for Rhenish wine, Neats-Tongues, &c. L. Herb. in Hen. 8. calls it the Stilly-art, but gives no reason for it.

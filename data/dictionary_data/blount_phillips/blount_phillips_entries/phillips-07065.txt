Lease, a word used in Common-law, and signifieth a demising, or letting of Lands, or Tenements, or right of a Rent, unto another for term of years, or of life, or for a Rent reserved, if it be in writing it is called a Lease by indenture, if by word of mouth, a Lease Parole, the party that letteth the Lease, is called the Leassour, the party that taketh it, the Leassee, being derived from the French word Laisser, i. to leave, or permit.

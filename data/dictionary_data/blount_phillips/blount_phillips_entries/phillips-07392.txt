Lycurgus, the son of Polydectes, and King of Sparta, after the death of his brother Eunomus, but he soon resigned up the Crown to Charilaus his brothers son, and having made wholesome laws for the good of the Common-wealth, which were confirmed by the approbation of the Delphic Oracle, he afterwards retired himself to Cyrrha, where at length he slew himself, and had a Temple built him, and Divine honours given bim by the Lacedaemonians: also the name of a King of Thrace, who proceeded so violently against Bacchus, that he forced him to retire himself to Naxus, and would have caused all the Vines in his Kingdom to be rooted up, that no sacrifice might be made to him, but ere he had effected his design he fell mad, and cut off his own legs.

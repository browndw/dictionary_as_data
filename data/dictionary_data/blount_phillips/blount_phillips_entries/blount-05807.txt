Eusebius Pamphilus (lib. 1. de vita Constantini) describes this peculiar Standard very curiously. The common form you may see in the 163 page of the Elements of Armories. In the Labarum these things are more. First, The Banner was of purple, where the pictures of the Emperor and his Children were wrought in gold, and stones of wonderful value and beauty; above the cross beam, or traverse Staff of the Banner stood the two first Greek Capitals of Christs name, and on the point or top of the Launce or Staff Imperial was advanced a Crown of gold set with precious stones. All in honor of his miraculous conversion upon the apparition of the Cross.

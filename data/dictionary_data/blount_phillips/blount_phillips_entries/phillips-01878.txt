Caerdiff, a Town in Glamorganshire, fortified by Robert Fitz-Hamon, whose Grandchild William Earle of Glocester, was afterwards besieged in the Castle, and taken prisoner by Yvor-Bach, a Brittish Mountaineir. In this Castle, Robert Curthose, son of William the Conqueror, after he was bereft of his eyes, lived till he was very old.

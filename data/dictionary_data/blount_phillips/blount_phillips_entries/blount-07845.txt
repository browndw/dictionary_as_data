Pervise (pervisum) a Bar; also a conference in former time called the Pervis among the young Councellors, Pleaders, Attorneys, or Students of the Law, such as at this day might resemble the course in the Inns of Court, or Chancery, called Moots and Bolts, wherein the form of pleading and arguing a case is exercised; for so doth Fortescu (chap. 51.) commending those Laws, prove, when he saith, That, after the Judges were risen at eleven of the clock, from hearing Causes at Westminster, Placitantes tunc se divertunt ad Pervisum, & alibi consulentes cum Servientibus ad legem & aliis Conciliariis suis.

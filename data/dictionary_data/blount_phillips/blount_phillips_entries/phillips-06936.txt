Lambith, q. Lomehith, i. e. a Lomy, or Clayish rode, a Town in Surry, famous for a stately Palace, belonging to the Arch-bishops of Canterbury, first built by Arch-bishop Baldwin, in the year 1183. in this place Hardy-Canute, the Danish King of England, giving up himself wholy to luxurious banquettings, & costly intertainments expired suddenly, in the midst of his debauchery, and excessive Cups.

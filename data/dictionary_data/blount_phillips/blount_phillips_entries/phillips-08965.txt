Penelope, the daughter of Icarius and the wife of Vlisses, she being careful to preserve her chastity in her husbands absence, being importuned by many suiters, who having been put off a great while, were at last resolved to ravish her; she desired onely so much time till she had made an end of what was upon her distaff, which when she had obtaind, she ravelled over night what she had spun in the day time, by which means she put them off till her husband returned home.

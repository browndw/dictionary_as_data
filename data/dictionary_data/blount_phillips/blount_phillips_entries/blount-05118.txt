Iesuats, an Order of Monks (begun at Siena in Italy by John Columbanus and Fran. Vincent, A. 1365) so called from their often having the name of Jesus in their mouths. Pope AErban the fifth approved them, and enjoyned them to wear a white garment, a white cover for their head, a leather girdle, and to go barefoot, using onely wooden soles; these were afterwards called Apostolici. Drantzius and Surius.

Misprision (from the Fr. Mespris, i. contempt, despising) signifies in our Law, negligence or over-sight. As for example Misprision of Treason or Felony, is a neglect or light account shewed of Treason or Felony committed, by not revealing it, when we know it to be committed. Stawnf. Pl. Cor. lib. 1. ca. 19. Misprision of Treason incurs imprisonment during the Kings pleasure, loss of goods and profits of lands during life. Misprision of Felony, seems onely finable by the Justices, before whom the party is attainted. Misprision signifies also a mistaking. 14. Ed. 3. Stat. 1. c. 6.

Riot (Fr. Riote, i. a bralling or contention) signifies the forcible doing an unlawful act, by three or more persons assembled together for that purpose. West. part. 2. Symbol. Tit. Indictments, Sect. 65. P. The differences and agreements between a Riot, Rout, and unlawful Assembly. See in Lamb. Eiren. lib. 2. cap. 5, &c. see the Stat. 1. Mar. 1. cap. 12. Kitch. fol. 19. and Cromptons Justice of Peace, fol. 53.

Lupeta cent al (Lat.) a place dedicated to the God Pan, from Lupa; because there a she-Wolf nourished Romulus and Remus. So the Sacrifices and Plays dedicated to Pan were called Lupercalia or lupercal Sacrifices, and the Priests of Pan (luperci) who, on the day of their Sacrifices ran up and down the City naked, and stroak the hands and bellies of women great with child, with a Goats skin, thereby to signifie both fruitfulness and easie deliverance. Rider.

Cambridge, the chief Town of Cambridge-shire, so called from a Bridge built over the River Cam: In this Town hath flourished for many ages, a famous University, consisting of sixteen Colledges, it hath been anciently reported that this Academy was founded by Cantabar a Spaniard, 375 years before Christ, and repaired by Sebert, King of the East Angles, in the year of our Lord 630. afterwards it was defaced by the Danes under Sueno, but being restored again by the Normans, it hath stood unviolated by War to this day.

Misseiden Messeito, or Me cidine, (from the Belg. Mistei) a plant having slender branches and green thick leaves, and never grows upon the ground, but upon other trees; the best is that which grows on the Oak; its leaves and fruit are good to soften and ripen any cold hard swellings. Of the berry of this vegetable, Birdlime is made. But that the Thrush eating these berries, and not able to digest them, and so from her inconverted muting this plant should arise (as the Antients afirmed) is doubted by Dr. Brown, fo. 98.

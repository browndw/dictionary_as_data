Lysimachus, the son of Agathocles, and one of the chief Captains of Alexander the Great, he was thrown to a Lyon to be devoured by Alexanders command, for hearing Calla cent a cent thenes the Philosopher after he was in Chains, but he wrapping his Garment about his hands, thrust them into the Lyons mouth, and pulling out his tongue killed him; after Alexanders death he had the Government of Thrace.

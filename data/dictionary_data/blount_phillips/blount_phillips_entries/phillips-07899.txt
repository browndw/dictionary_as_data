Mithridates, a King of Pontus, who spake 22 Languages, he rebelling against the Romans was overcome by Sylla near Dardanus, and afterwards by Lucullus near Cyzicus, and flying to Tigranes King of Armenia he renewed the war, but at length was totally overthrown by Pompey, and besieged in his own Palace, where having in vain attempted to poison himself, he assisted Gallus the Executioner (when his hand trembled) in the murdering of himself. He was the first inventour of that excellent Antidote against infection and poison, called from his own name Mithridate.

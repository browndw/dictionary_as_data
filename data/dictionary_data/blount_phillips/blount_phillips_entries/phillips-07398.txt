Lynceus, the son of Apareus, and one of the Argonauts, he was reported to be so quick-sighted, that he could see through stone walls even to the very deep it self, and that he could discern the Moon in her last quarter, and the first, the very same day, in the signe of Aries; whence a sharp-sighted man is proverbially called a Lynceus, also Lynceus and Idas were two brothers, who fought with Castor and Pollux, about the two daughters of Leucippus, Castor fell by the hand of Lynceus, Lynceus by the hand of Pollux, Idas going about to slay Pollux, was strook with Thunder from Heaven.

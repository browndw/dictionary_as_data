Sma cent ctyma cent uans, about the beginning of the long Parliament in the year 1641, five Ministers wrote a book against Episcopacy and the Common Prayer, in behalf of the Presbyterian Government, to which they all subscribed their names, being a cent tephen Marshal, Edm. Calamy, Tho. Young, Matth. Newcomen, and Will Spurstow; the first letters whereof make this word, Smectymnuus; and from thence they and their Followers were called Smectymnuans.

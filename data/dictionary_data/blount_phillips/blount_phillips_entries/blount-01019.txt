Auditor (Lat.) one that hearkens; But in the common acceptation it is an Officer to some great personage who does yearly (by examining the accounts of all Under-officers accountable) make up a general book, that shews the difference between their receipts and their allowances, commonly called allocations; As namely, the Auditors of the Exchequer take the Accounts of those Receivers, which receive the revenues of the Augmentation, as also of the Sheriffs, Escheators, Collectors, and Customers, and set them down and perfect them. See Stat. 33 H. 8. cap. 33.

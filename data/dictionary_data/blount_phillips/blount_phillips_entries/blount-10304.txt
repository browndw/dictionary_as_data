Taberd or Tabard (Sax.) a Jacket, Jerkin, Mandilion, or sleeveless Coat; a chymere Verstegan says, it was antienta cent y a short Gown that reached no further then the midleg; Stow in his Survey, p. 456. says, 'twas a Jacket or sleeveless Coat, whole before, open on both sides, with a square Collar winged at the shoulders. It is now the name onely of an Heralds Coat, and is called their Coat of Arms in Service. It is also the sign of an antient Inn in Southwark.

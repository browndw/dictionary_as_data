Ordinary, (lat.) in the Civil-law, signifieth any judge that hath authority to take knowledge of causes in his own right, as he is a Magistrate, and not by deputation, in Common-law it is usually taken for him that hath ordinary Jurisdiction in Causes Ecclesiastical; also in Heraldry, Ordinaries are those charges that by a certain property do belong, to that art, and are of ordinary use therein, they are also called proper charges.

Palamedes, the son of Nauplius King of Eubaea, he was the occasion of Vlisses being forced against his will to go to the Trojan wars, but was himself by a forged accusation of Vlisses, stoned to death by the Graecian Army. He is said to have been the first that accomodated the year to the course of the Sun; he is also said to have found out the use of weights and measures, and to have added to the Greek tongue these 4 letters, a in non-Latin alphabet a.

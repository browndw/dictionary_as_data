Tamworth, a Town so called from the River Tame, and the Saxon word Weordh, which signifieth a Barton, Ferm-house, or River Island; it is situate partly in Warwick-shire, partly in Stafford-shire, and was anciently the chief residence of the Kings of the Mercians. this Town after it had suffered much by the Danish Wars, was repaired by Aethelfleda, Lady of the Mercians; also Edith King Eadgar's sister founded here a Religious house for veiled Virgins.

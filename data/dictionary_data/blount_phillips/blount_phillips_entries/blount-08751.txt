Rebus (Lat.) Whereas (says Camden) Poesie is a speaking Picture, and Picture a speechless Poesie, they who wanted wit to express their conceit in speech, did use to depaint it out as it were in Pictures, which they called Rebus, by a Latin name, well fitting their devise. These our English in Edward the Thirds time, learnt of the pregnant Picardes, and were so well liked and entertained here by all degrees, that he was no body that could not hammer out of his name an invention by this wit-craft.

Parisyllabical (parisyllabicus) that hath equal syllables; those words are called Parisyllabical, which have no more syllables in one then another, as fama, mensa, &c. So likewise we say in Grammar, the first declea cent sion of Nouns is Parisyllabique, because all the Cases of such Nouns in the singular number especially have even syllables, as Gemma, gemmae, gemmae, gemmam, gemma, gemma, &c.

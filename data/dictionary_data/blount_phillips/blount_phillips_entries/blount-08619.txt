Quarentine (from the Fr. Quarantaine) is a benefit allowed by the Law of England, to the Widow of a landed man deceased, whereby she may challenge to continue in his chief Mansion house by the space of forty days after his decease. Of this see Bract. l. 2. cap. 40. And if the Heir or any other attempt to eject her, she may have the Writ de Quarantina habenda. Fitz. nat. br. fol. 161.

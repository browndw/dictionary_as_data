Benedictines or Benedictine Monks, a sort of Religious persons so called, from St. Benedict, who was born at Nursia in AEmbria, An. 482. he gathered the Monks of Italy together, and gave them a rule in writing. Their habit is a loose Gown of black, their under Garment white woollen, their Crowns shaven. Of this antient Order have been above fifty Popes, and at least 200 Cardinals, &c.

Palindromes (Gr.) are those sentences or verses, where the syllables are the same backward as forward. As a noble Lady in Queen Elizabeths days, being for a time forbidden the Court, for too much familiarity with a great Lord in favour, gave this Devise, the Moon covered with a cloud, and underneath this Palindrome for Motto, Ablata, at alba.Roma tibi subito motibus ibit amor.And this in English, which is more difficult to compose;Lewd did I live and evil I did dwel.

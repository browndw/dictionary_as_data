Collation, (lat.) a joyning or comparing together, also a Banquet; also Collation of a benefice is the bestowing of a benefice by the Bishop who hath it in his own gift or patronage; whereas the institution into a benefice is performed by the Bishop at the presentation of another who is Patron of the place, or hath a Patrons right. It is moreover a term used by Booksellers, and signifies a looking upon the letters at the bottom of every page to see that the book be perfect.

Ordeal, (a Sax. word, signifying judgement) a kinde of purgation practised in ancient times, whereby the party purged was judged free from Crime, it is called in Common-law purgatio vulgaris, of this purgation there are several kindes; as Camp-fight, wherein he that overcame in single Combat, was judged to have the right on his side. Fire-Ordeal, i. a passing blindfold with bare feet over red hot plough-shares, and Water-Ordeal, i. e. a putting ones arms up to the elbows in seething water.

Lincolns-Inn, one of the four Inns of Court, and in antiquity next the Temples; it was for the most part purchased of Sir Edw. Suliard of Essex, by the Benchers and Gentlemen of that house; But it took denomination from Sir H. Lacy Earl of Lincoln; to whom part of this House was given by Edw. the first. See more in Stow. Chron. p. 1072.

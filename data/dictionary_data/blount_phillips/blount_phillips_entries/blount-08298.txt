Pretorian (praetorius) of or belonging to, or attending on a Praetor; the Pretorian guard of Soldiers in old Rome were first established by Augustus, and consisted of Ten thousand, every one having double the wages of a Legionary Soldier, and were in the same nature to the Emperors, as the Janizaries are to the Great Turk, or as the Mamalukes were to the Sultans of Egypt.

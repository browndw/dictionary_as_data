Siza cent , is a farthings worth of bread or drink, which Schollars in Cambridge have at the Buttery, noted with the letter S. As in Oxford with the letter Q. for half a farthing, and Qa. for a farthing; And whereas they say in Oxford, to Battel in the Buttery Book i. to set down on their names, what they take in Bread, Drink, Butter, Cheese, &c. In Cambrigde they call it Sizing.

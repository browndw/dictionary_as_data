Chancellour, from the Latin word Cancelli, Latices, with which in former time the judgement seats were compassed; it is a title of honour given unto him, who is the chief man next unto the Prince for matter of justice in civil affairs, having power to moderate, and temper the written Law, according to equity, also the Chancellour of the Exchequer is a supream Officer, appointed to moderate the extreamities in Exchequer.

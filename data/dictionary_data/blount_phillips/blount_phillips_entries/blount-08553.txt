Purine (from the Fr. pur, i purus, and lieu, i. locus) is all that ground near any Forest, which being made Forest by Hen. 2. Ric. 1. or King John, was, by perambulations granted by Hen. 3. severed again from the same. In which Purlue it was lawful for the owner of the ground to hunt, if he could dispend forty shillings per annum of Free-hold, &c. See Manwood part. 1. p. 151.157.

Perquisite (perquisitum) signifies in Bracton any thing purchased, as Perquisitum facere, lib. 2. ca. 30. num. 3. and lib. 4. cap. 22. Perquisites of Courts are those profits, that accrew to the Lord of a Mannor, by vertue of his Court Baron, over and above the certain and yearly rents of his Land, as Fines for Copy-Holds, Waifs, Estraies, and such like.

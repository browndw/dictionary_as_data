Mesentery (mesenterium) the middle of the bowels and entrals, a certain thick and fat skin; or the double skin that fastens the bowels to the back, and each to other, and also wraps and encloses a number of veins (called Meseraick or Mesenterick Veins) being branches of the great carrying vein, by which both the guts are nourished, and the juyce of the meat concocted, is conveyed to the Liver to be made blood.
